Safety

SAR limits, first trimester considerations, and gadolinium contrast

SAR at 3 T
  • Maximum recommended total exam duration: 1 hour
  • SAR ≤ 2.0 W/kg — no known issues
  • SAR 2.0–2.5 W/kg — use with caution
  • Severe polyhydramnios or fetal hydrops: prefer 1.5 T to reduce standing-wave artefacts; if 3 T is used, setting the B1 shim to adaptive partially mitigates this
First Trimester
  • No established evidence of fetal harm from MRI; however, data remain limited
  • Precautionary approach recommended — restrict to cases with a clear maternal or fetal clinical indication
Gadolinium Contrast
  • Published data indicate a small increased risk of intrauterine fetal death; weigh against the clinical benefit for the mother on an individual basis
