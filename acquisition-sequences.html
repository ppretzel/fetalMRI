Sequences

Sequence types, protocols, and workflow notes

Sequence Types
  • bFFE / TrueFISP / FIESTA (balanced SSFP) — primary workhorse; high SNR, good soft-tissue contrast; sFFP variants tend to elicit less fetal movement than other sequence types
  • HASTE / SSFSE (half-Fourier single-shot TSE) — robust T2 weighting, highly resistant to motion
  • GRE T1 (gradient-echo) — T1 contrast; myelination assessment, haemorrhage, liver signal, meconium
  • EPI DWI (echo-planar diffusion-weighted) — abdominal organ characterisation; normal kidneys appear diffusion-restricted
Workflow
  • Frequent replanning between acquisitions is intrinsic to the workflow — effective scan time is shorter than total exam duration
  • Even in motion-degraded sequences, review all available data: pathology visible in a blurred series may be absent from the cleaner ones
